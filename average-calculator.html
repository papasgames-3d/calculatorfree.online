Examples & Tips

Example 1: Test Scores

Numbers: 85, 92, 78, 96, 89, 85

Mean: (85+92+78+96+89+85) ÷ 6 = 87.5

Median: 85, 85, 89, 92, 96 → (87) = 87

Mode: 85 (appears twice)

Range: 96 - 78 = 18

Example 2: Sales Data

Numbers: 1200, 1500, 1100, 1800, 1300

Mean: (1200+1500+1100+1800+1300) ÷ 5 = 1380

Median: 1100, 1200, 1300, 1500, 1800 → 1300

Mode: No mode (all values unique)

Range: 1800 - 1100 = 700

Quick Tips:

  • Mean: Best for normally distributed data
  • Median: Better for skewed data or with outliers
  • Mode: Useful for categorical data or finding most common value
  • Range: Shows the spread of your data
  • You can enter decimal numbers, negative numbers, and zero
  • Use comma, space, or new line to separate multiple numbers
